## 3. Oil Tanker Market

### 3.1 VLCC (Very Large Crude Carriers) Rates

**All-Time High:** $423,736/day (reached after Feb. 28 conflict began)
- February 2026 pre-crisis: Spot earnings past $150,000/day
- Late February Baltic TCE: $177,000/day
- Worldscale: 700 points (March, post-conflict)

**Historical Context:** This represents the highest VLCC rates ever recorded, surpassing previous records set during other geopolitical crises.

### 3.2 Suezmax Rates

**Late February Baltic TCE:** $129,000/day
- Rates hitting year-to-date highs as market tightens globally
- ~15% of Suezmax fleet migrated to shadow/restricted fleet

### 3.3 Aframax Rates

**Late February Baltic TCE:** $78,000/day
- ~25% of Aframax/LR2 tonnage in shadow or restricted fleet
- Rates coincide with geopolitical stress levels historically

### 3.4 Fleet and Market Structure

**Shadow Fleet Impact:**
- Over 15% of total tanker tonnage in "shadow fleet" servicing sanctioned trades
- ~120 vessels consolidated under South Korea's Sinokor and MSC-linked buyers
- Effectively removed from mainstream compliant market

**War Risk Insurance:** Major insurers dropped war risk protection for Middle East transits, sending insurance premiums to unprecedented levels

### 3.5 Strait of Hormuz Traffic Data

**Pre-Conflict:** 100+ ships daily transiting the strait
**Post-Conflict (since Feb. 28):**
- Only 21 tankers transited since war began (per S&P Global Market Intelligence)
- ~90 ships total (including all vessel types) crossed since conflict started
- ~22 vessels carrying crude, LPG, and LNG remained anchored awaiting safe passage
- Many engaged in "dark" transits evading Western sanctions

**Strait Significance:**
- Handles ~20% of global oil supply
- 38% of seaborne crude trade
- Near-standstill described as largest oil supply disruption since 1970s energy crisis

**US Policy:** Treasury Secretary Bessent stated US is allowing Iranian oil tankers through the strait
