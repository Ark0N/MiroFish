## Market Context Summary

### Critical Signals (March 2026)

1. **Oil Price Shock in Progress**: WTI surged 42% and Brent 42% in 3 weeks (late Feb to mid-Mar 2026). Brent has breached $100/BBL. This is the most rapid price increase since the Russia-Ukraine invasion spike of 2022.

2. **Gasoline Following Crude**: Pump prices jumped from $3.015 to $3.720/gal in two weeks, but still lag the crude price move -- suggesting further consumer price increases ahead.

3. **US Production Near Record**: Despite the price spike, US production was at 13,655 MBBL/D in December 2025 (near the October 2025 record of 13,864). This suggests the price spike is NOT caused by US supply issues.

4. **Natural Gas Decoupled**: Henry Hub gas prices remain stable at ~$3.00-3.27/MMBTU, far below the Feb 4 cold-snap spike of $6.88. The oil-gas divergence suggests oil-specific supply disruption rather than broad energy crisis.

5. **Global Supply Concentration Risk**: The US and Russia produce ~57% of world oil. OPEC controls another ~30%. Any geopolitical event disrupting Russian or Middle Eastern supply creates outsized price impact.

6. **Import Dependency Shift**: US has diversified away from Middle East dependency -- Western Hemisphere sources now provide 72% of imports. Canada alone supplies 53%.

7. **Maritime Indicators**: 3,897 tankers tracked globally with notable concentration at Rotterdam. Tanker positioning patterns can signal trade flow changes.
