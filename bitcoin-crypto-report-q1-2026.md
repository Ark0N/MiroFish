## 3. The Three Catalysts for $120K+

The bull case ($120K-$180K) requires all three of these to materialize:

### Catalyst 1: Pension Fund Adoption (76% likely)
Pension funds entering Bitcoin through spot ETFs would bring massive, patient capital with long time horizons. Early signs exist — 60% of institutional investors surveyed plan to increase crypto allocation in 2026.

### Catalyst 2: Fed Rate Cuts (76% likely)
Current Fed funds rate: 3.75-4.00%. Market prices 2-3 additional 25bp cuts. Lower rates reduce the opportunity cost of holding Bitcoin vs. cash/bonds. Global M2 money supply is expanding — historically correlated with BTC appreciation.

### Catalyst 3: Sovereign Bitcoin Reserve (55% likely)
The most uncertain but highest-impact catalyst. El Salvador has expanded to Bitcoin-backed bonds. Several nations are exploring reserves. A major economy announcing a strategic BTC reserve would trigger cascading institutional FOMO.

## 4. The Bear Case ($50K-$80K, ~20% probability)

The simulation also identified clear downside risks:

- **Inflation resurgence** forcing a hawkish Fed reversal — the single biggest threat
- **Custodian failure** — Coinbase custodies multiple ETFs; a breach would trigger synchronized redemptions
- **Regulatory crackdown** — IRS tightening reporting; stablecoin legislation could go either way
- **Recession** driving broad risk-off sentiment

### The Stablecoin Risk
Tether ($140B market cap) is the liquidity backbone of global crypto. Any loss of confidence in USDT reserves could evaporate market liquidity. Circle (USDC) pursuing an IPO ties stablecoin stability to traditional market sentiment.

### Concentration Risk
The irony: Bitcoin was designed to eliminate trusted intermediaries, but institutional adoption has re-centralized custody. A handful of custodians now control enormous BTC positions. This is a new systemic risk that didn't exist in previous cycles.
