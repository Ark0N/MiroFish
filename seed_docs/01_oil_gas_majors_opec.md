# Global Oil & Gas Industry Landscape: Majors, OPEC+, and National Oil Companies (March 2026)

## International Oil Companies (IOCs) — The Supermajors

### ExxonMobil
ExxonMobil Corporation, led by CEO Darren Woods, remains the world's largest publicly traded oil company by market capitalization. The company's upstream division, headed by Executive VP Liam Mallon, operates major assets including the Permian Basin holdings in Texas and New Mexico, the Guyana Stabroek Block (operated with partners Hess Corporation and CNOOC), and deepwater Gulf of Mexico platforms including Hoover-Diana and Thunder Horse. ExxonMobil's downstream president Karen McKee oversees refining at Baytown Refinery (Texas), Baton Rouge Refinery (Louisiana), Beaumont Refinery (Texas), Fawley Refinery (UK), Rotterdam Refinery (Netherlands), Antwerp Refinery (Belgium), Singapore Refinery, and Fujian Refinery (China JV with Sinopec and Saudi Aramco). ExxonMobil Chemical Company produces polyethylene, polypropylene, and specialty chemicals at facilities in Baytown, Baton Rouge, and Singapore. CFO Kathryn Mikells manages the company's $30B+ annual capex budget. The company's Pioneer Natural Resources acquisition (closed 2024) added ~700,000 boe/d of Permian production under integration lead VP Scott Sheffield's former assets managed by Rich Dealy.

### Chevron
Chevron Corporation, under CEO Mike Wirth, is the second-largest US oil company. Chevron's upstream chief VP Jeff Gustavson manages operations in the Permian Basin (DJ Basin and San Joaquin Valley), offshore Gulf of Mexico (Jack/St. Malo, Tahiti, Big Foot platforms), Australia's Gorgon LNG and Wheatstone LNG projects, and Kazakhstan's Tengizchevroil (TCO) expansion led by project director Andrew MacKenzie. Chevron's midstream operations include a 50% stake in GS Caltex with GS Energy in South Korea. Downstream EVP Mark Nelson oversees Pascagoula Refinery (Mississippi), El Segundo Refinery (California), Richmond Refinery (California), and the Caltex brand across Asia-Pacific. Chevron's pending Hess Corporation acquisition (CEO John Hess) would add a 30% stake in Guyana's Stabroek block, but faces arbitration by CNOOC at the ICC. Chevron New Energies president Jeff Gustavson also leads hydrogen and CCS ventures, including the Bayou Bend CCS project with Talos Energy and Carbonvert.

### Shell plc
Shell plc, led by CEO Wael Sawan, is Europe's largest oil company headquartered in London. Shell's Integrated Gas division, headed by Steve Hill, manages the world's largest LNG trading portfolio, including equity stakes in QatarEnergy's North Field expansion, Australia's Prelude FLNG (the world's largest floating facility), Nigeria LNG (with NNPC, TotalEnergies, and Eni), Sakhalin II (transferred to Sakhalin Energy after Russia exit), and the LNG Canada project in Kitimat, British Columbia (joint venture with Petronas, PetroChina, Mitsubishi, and Korea Gas Corporation KOGAS). Shell's upstream director Zoe Yujnovich oversees deepwater assets in the Gulf of Mexico (Appomattox, Vito, Perdido), Nigeria (SPDC JV), Oman (Block 6 with Petroleum Development Oman), and Brazil's pre-salt Santos Basin (Libra consortium with Petrobras, TotalEnergies, CNOOC, and CNPC). Shell's downstream president Huibert Vigeveno runs Pernis Refinery (Netherlands, Europe's largest), Rhineland Refinery (Germany), Pulau Bukom Refinery (Singapore), Deer Park Refinery (Texas), and Scotford Refinery (Alberta). Shell Chemicals produces ethylene and propylene at Moerdijk (Netherlands), Norco (Louisiana), and the Shell Polymers Monaca plant in Pennsylvania.

### BP plc
BP plc, under CEO Murray Auchincloss, operates across the entire energy value chain. BP's production & operations EVP Gordon Birrell manages assets including the Thunder Horse platform (GoM, 50/50 with ExxonMobil), Atlantis and Mad Dog platforms (GoM), Azeri-Chirag-Gunashli (ACG) field in Azerbaijan (with SOCAR, Inpex, Equinor, and Lukoil), Rumaila oil field in Iraq (with PetroChina and Basra Oil Company), the Greater Tortue Ahmeyim LNG project offshore Mauritania and Senegal (with Kosmos Energy and SMH), and BP's Oman Block 61 (Khazzan tight gas project). BP Trading, led by Carol Alexander, is one of the world's largest physical oil and gas traders. BP's refining division operates Whiting Refinery (Indiana, largest in the US Midwest), Cherry Point Refinery (Washington), Toledo Refinery (Ohio, JV with Cenovus Energy), Castellon Refinery (Spain), Rotterdam Refinery (Netherlands), and Gelsenkirchen Refinery (Germany). BP's Lightsource bp solar JV is a top-10 global solar developer.

### TotalEnergies SE
TotalEnergies SE, headed by CEO Patrick Pouyanne, is France's energy giant with significant LNG exposure. E&P president Nicolas Terraz manages upstream operations in Angola (Block 17, Block 32 with Sonangol), Nigeria (OML 58, OML 99 with NNPC), the North Sea (Elgin-Franklin, Culzean), Republic of Congo (Moho Nord), Libya (Mabruk, Al-Jurf with NOC), Papua New Guinea LNG (with Oil Search/Santos, and ExxonMobil), and Mozambique LNG (Area 1, delayed due to insurgency but partnered with ONGC, ENH, Mitsui, PTTEP, and Bharat Petroleum). TotalEnergies' Gas division VP Laurent Vivier leads the Ichthys LNG project (Australia, with Inpex as operator), Qatar NFE participation, and the Cameron LNG facility in Louisiana. TotalEnergies' Refining & Chemicals chief Bernard Pinatel manages Normandy Refinery (Gonfreville), Donges Refinery, Feyzin Refinery, Antwerp Refinery, Leuna Refinery (Germany), and Port Arthur Refinery (Texas, JV with Saudi Aramco via Motiva Enterprises). Renewables chief Stephane Michel leads 40 GW+ of solar and wind portfolio.

### ConocoPhillips
ConocoPhillips, led by CEO Ryan Lance, is the world's largest independent E&P company. COO Dominic Macklon manages a Permian Basin position of ~1 million boe/d, Alaskan North Slope operations (Kuparuk River, Alpine, Willow project controversially approved), Lower 48 assets in the Eagle Ford (Texas) and Bakken (North Dakota), Norwegian North Sea operations, and LNG exposure through APLNG (Australia, JV with Origin Energy and Sinopec), Darwin LNG, and a 15% stake in Qatar's NFE. The Marathon Oil Corporation acquisition (CEO Lee Tillman) added Eagle Ford and Bakken positions. CFO Bill Bullock manages the company's balance sheet with shareholder returns via variable buybacks.

### Eni SpA
Eni SpA, under CEO Claudio Descalzi, is Italy's national champion. Upstream chief Alessandro Puliti manages operations in Libya (with NOC), Egypt (Zohr gas field in the Mediterranean, the largest ever discovered there), Mozambique (Coral South FLNG with ExxonMobil, CNPC, KOGAS, and ENH), Nigeria (NAOC JV with NNPC), Congo Brazzaville, Kazakhstan (Karachaganak, Kashagan with Shell, TotalEnergies, KazMunayGas, and consortium partners), Algeria (with Sonatrach), and Indonesia (Merakes gas, East Kalimantan). Eni's Plenitude subsidiary (CEO Stefano Goberti) combines retail gas/power with 8 GW renewables. Eni Versalis (CEO Adriano Alfani) is Italy's largest petrochemical group.

### Equinor ASA
Equinor ASA, led by CEO Anders Opedal, is Norway's state-controlled energy company (67% owned by the Norwegian government). Equinor's E&P chief Kjetil Hove manages the massive Johan Sverdrup field (North Sea, 720,000 bbl/d at peak), Troll gas field (Europe's largest gas reserve), Snohvit LNG (Hammerfest), Peregrino (Brazil), Bay du Nord (Canada), and Bacalhau (Brazil pre-salt, with ExxonMobil). Equinor's Marketing, Midstream & Processing division, led by Irene Rummelhoff, manages Europe's largest gas trading book. Equinor Renewables chief Pal Eitrheim leads the Dogger Bank offshore wind farm (UK, world's largest, with SSE plc) and Empire Wind (New York).

## OPEC+ Members and Leadership

### OPEC Secretariat
The Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC), headquartered in Vienna, Austria, is led by Secretary General Haitham Al Ghais. OPEC's research director Ayed Al-Qahtani provides market analysis underpinning production decisions.

### Saudi Arabia / Saudi Aramco
Saudi Arabia, the world's largest oil exporter, is led by Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman (MBS), with Energy Minister Prince Abdulaziz bin Salman setting oil policy. Saudi Aramco, the world's most valuable company, is led by CEO Amin Nasser. Aramco's upstream chief Nasir Al-Naimi manages the Ghawar field (world's largest, ~3.8 million bbl/d capacity), Safaniyah (world's largest offshore field), Shaybah, Khurais, Manifa, and Berri fields. Aramco Trading Company, led by Ibrahim Al-Buainain, handles global oil marketing. Aramco's downstream chief Mohammed Al Qahtani oversees Ras Tanura Refinery (largest in the Middle East), Yanbu Refinery, Jazan Refinery, SATORP (Jubail, JV with TotalEnergies), and SASREF. Aramco's Jafurah unconventional gas development, led by SVP Ahmad Al-Sa'adi, targets 2 bcf/d of sales gas to reduce domestic oil burning. Saudi Aramco has partnerships with S-Oil (South Korea), Motiva Enterprises (US), and HAPCO (China, with Zhejiang Petrochemical).

### United Arab Emirates / ADNOC
The UAE, led by President Sheikh Mohamed bin Zayed Al Nahyan, pursues an aggressive oil expansion strategy. ADNOC (Abu Dhabi National Oil Company), headed by CEO Sultan Ahmed Al Jaber (who also serves as UAE climate envoy), manages the Upper Zakum, Lower Zakum, ADMA-OPCO, and Bab onshore fields. ADNOC's upstream director Yaser Saeed Almazrouei leads the 5-million-bbl/d capacity target. ADNOC Gas (CEO Ahmed Alebri) operates the Habshan-Ruwais gas complex and the Ruwais LNG project. ADNOC Refining (CEO Abdulaziz Alhajri) runs the Ruwais Refinery and Abu Dhabi Refinery. ADNOC Distribution (CEO Bader Al Lamki) operates 800+ fuel stations. ADNOC's Borouge petrochemical JV with Borealis (CEO Thomas Gangl) is a major polyolefins producer.

### Iraq
Iraq, led by PM Mohammed Shia al-Sudani, with Oil Minister Hayan Abdel-Ghani, produces ~4.5 million bbl/d. Iraq's Basra Oil Company (director Basim Abdul Karim) manages southern super-giant fields. SOMO (State Organization for Marketing of Oil), headed by Alaa Al-Yasseri, handles all Iraqi crude exports. Key fields: Rumaila (BP, PetroChina), West Qurna-2 (Lukoil), Majnoon (formerly Shell, now Basra Oil), Halfaya (PetroChina, TotalEnergies, Petronas), and Zubair (Eni, Occidental).

### Kuwait / KPC
Kuwait, led by Emir Sheikh Meshal Al Ahmad, with Oil Minister Saad Al-Barrak, produces ~2.7 million bbl/d. Kuwait Petroleum Corporation (KPC), headed by CEO Sheikh Nawaf Al-Sabah, manages Kuwait Oil Company (KOC, CEO Ahmad Al-Eidan) for upstream, Kuwait National Petroleum Company (KNPC) for refining (Al-Ahmadi and Mina Abdullah refineries, plus the new $16B Al-Zour Refinery), Kuwait Integrated Petroleum Industries Company (KIPIC, CEO Waleed Al-Bader) for the Al-Zour complex, and Petrochemical Industries Company (PIC, CEO Mutlaq Al-Azmi).

### Iran / NIOC
Iran, under President Masoud Pezeshkian, with Oil Minister Mohsen Paknejad, faces sanctions limiting production to ~3.2 million bbl/d. National Iranian Oil Company (NIOC), headed by Mohsen Khojasteh-Mehr, manages the South Pars gas field (world's largest, shared with Qatar's North Field), Azadegan oil field, Yadavaran (developed with CNPC), and Masjed Soleyman. NIOC subsidiaries include National Iranian Gas Company (NIGC, CEO Majid Chegeni), National Petrochemical Company (NPC, CEO Morteza Shahmirzaei), and Naftiran Intertrade Company (NICO). Iran's sanctions evasion network involves ship-to-ship transfers and Chinese teapot refineries.

### Russia (OPEC+ Partner)
Russia, under President Vladimir Putin, with Deputy PM Alexander Novak handling energy policy, remains a top-3 oil producer at ~9.5 million bbl/d. Key companies:
- **Rosneft** (CEO Igor Sechin): Russia's largest, operates Samotlor, Priobskoye, Vostok Oil (Taymyr Peninsula mega-project), and Vankor fields. Rosneft's Tuapse and Komsomolsk refineries serve domestic demand.
- **Lukoil** (CEO Vadim Vorobyov, succeeding the late Vagit Alekperov as chairman): Russia's largest private oil company, operates West Qurna-2 (Iraq), Volga-Urals fields, and refineries including Nizhny Novgorod, Perm, and ISAB (Sicily, Italy). Trading arm Litasco SA operates from Geneva.
- **Gazprom** (CEO Alexey Miller, Chairman Viktor Zubkov): World's largest natural gas producer, manages the Bovanenkovo, Urengoy, and Yamburg mega-fields. Gazprom Export (CEO Elena Burmistrova) handles pipeline gas sales to Europe (via remaining Nord Stream capacity and TurkStream). Gazprom Neft (CEO Alexander Dyukov) is a major oil producer (~800,000 bbl/d).
- **Novatek** (CEO Leonid Mikhelson, board member Gennady Timchenko): Russia's largest independent gas producer, operates Yamal LNG (partnered with TotalEnergies, CNPC, and Silk Road Fund) and the troubled Arctic LNG 2 (facing sanctions, partners include CNPC, CNOOC, and a Japan consortium of Mitsui and JOGMEC).
- **Surgutneftegas** (CEO Vladimir Bogdanov): Famously opaque company sitting on $60B+ cash reserves.
- **Tatneft** (CEO Nail Maganov): Tatarstan's oil champion, operates TANECO Refinery.

### Nigeria / NNPC
Nigeria, under President Bola Tinubu, with Petroleum Minister Heineken Lokpobiri, is Africa's largest oil producer. NNPC Limited (CEO Mele Kyari) manages JVs with Shell (SPDC), ExxonMobil (NNPC/MPN), Chevron (NNPC/CNL), TotalEnergies (NNPC/TEPNG), and Eni (NAOC). The Dangote Refinery (founder Aliko Dangote, Africa's richest person), located in Lekki Free Zone, Lagos, is a 650,000 bbl/d single-train refinery — the world's largest — now fully operational and processing Nigerian Bonny Light, Forcados, Escravos, and Qua Iboe crudes. NLNG (Nigeria LNG) at Bonny Island, managed by CEO Philip Mshelbila, is a JV of NNPC, Shell, TotalEnergies, and Eni.

### Other OPEC Members
- **Venezuela**: President Nicolas Maduro, PDVSA president Pedro Tellechea, producing ~900,000 bbl/d under US sanctions. Orinoco Belt heavy oil JVs with Chevron (Petropiar, Petroboscan), Repsol (Petroquiriquire), and Eni/Repsol (PetroSucre). PDVSA subsidiary CITGO Petroleum (US refineries in Lake Charles, Lemont, Corpus Christi) under legal battles.
- **Libya**: NOC (National Oil Company) chairman Farhat Bengdara manages volatile production (~1.1 million bbl/d when stable). Key fields: Sharara (operated by Repsol JV Akakus Oil), El Feel (Eni/Mellitah Oil & Gas), Es Sider terminal, Ras Lanuf Refinery.
- **Algeria**: Sonatrach (CEO Rachid Hachichi) is Africa's largest company by revenue. Manages Hassi Messaoud oil field, Hassi R'Mel gas field (world's third largest), and the Arzew LNG complex. Partners include TotalEnergies, Eni, Repsol, Occidental, and Cepsa.
- **Angola**: Sonangol (CEO Sebastiao Gaspar Martins) manages deepwater blocks operated by TotalEnergies, ExxonMobil, BP, Chevron, and Eni. Angola LNG in Soyo processes associated gas.
- **Ecuador**: Petroecuador (CEO Jorge Luis Lopez) manages the ITT-Yasuni fields despite a 2023 referendum.
- **Congo**: SNPC (Societe Nationale des Petroles du Congo) works with TotalEnergies and Eni on offshore projects.
- **Gabon**: GOC (Gabon Oil Company) partnered with BW Energy (CEO Carl Arnet) and Perenco.

### OPEC+ Non-OPEC Partners
- **Kazakhstan**: KazMunayGas (CEO Magzum Mirzagaliyev) manages stakes in Tengiz (Chevron-led TCO), Kashagan (NCOC consortium: Shell, TotalEnergies, Eni, ExxonMobil, CNPC, Inpex, KMG), and Karachaganak (KPO: Shell, Eni, Chevron, Lukoil, KMG).
- **Mexico**: Pemex (CEO Octavio Romero Oropeza) manages Cantarell decline and Dos Bocas Refinery (Olmeca). Mexico's Secretaria de Energia (SENER) regulates. Partners include Talos Energy, Harbour Energy, and Wintershall Dea.
- **Azerbaijan**: SOCAR (CEO Rovshan Najaf) manages the ACG complex (BP-led), Shah Deniz gas field (BP, TPAO, LUKOIL, NICO, SOCAR), and the Southern Gas Corridor (TANAP pipeline to Turkey, TAP pipeline to Italy).
- **Oman**: OQ Group (CEO Mulham Al-Jarf) manages PDO (Petroleum Development Oman, 60% government, Shell 34%), the Duqm Refinery (JV with Kuwait's KIPIC), and Oman LNG.
- **Bahrain**: BAPCO Energies (CEO Mark Thomas) operates the expanded Sitra Refinery and the Bahrain Field (oldest producing field in the Gulf).
- **Brunei**: PetroleumBRUNEI and Brunei Shell Petroleum (BSP) manage declining onshore/offshore production.
- **Malaysia**: Petronas (CEO Muhammad Taufik) — see LNG section.
- **South Sudan**: Nile Petroleum (Dar Petroleum consortium with CNPC, Petronas, Sinopec, and Tri-Ocean Energy).

## Key Industry Associations and Regulatory Bodies

- **International Energy Agency (IEA)**: Executive Director Fatih Birol publishes monthly Oil Market Reports and the flagship World Energy Outlook.
- **US Energy Information Administration (EIA)**: Administrator Joe DeCarolis publishes Short-Term Energy Outlook (STEO) and weekly petroleum status reports.
- **OPEC Secretariat Research Division**: Director Ayed Al-Qahtani publishes the Monthly Oil Market Report (MOMR).
- **US Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC)**: Chairman Willie Phillips regulates interstate energy infrastructure.
- **US Department of Energy (DOE)**: Secretary Jennifer Granholm manages the Strategic Petroleum Reserve (SPR) at Bayou Choctaw, Big Hill, Bryan Mound, and West Hackberry sites.
- **European Commission DG Energy**: Commissioner Kadri Simson leads EU energy policy including REPowerEU.
- **Japan Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI)**: Minister oversees JOGMEC (Japan Oil, Gas and Metals National Corporation) strategic reserves and E&P investments.
- **India Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas**: Minister Hardeep Singh Puri oversees ONGC, Oil India Limited (OIL), Indian Oil Corporation (IOC), Bharat Petroleum (BPCL), and Hindustan Petroleum (HPCL).
