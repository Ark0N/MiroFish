# Connected Markets: Petrochemicals, Refining, Shipping, Utilities, and Supply Chain (March 2026)

## Petrochemical Industry (Directly Connected to Gas/Oil Feedstock)

### Global Petrochemical Giants
- **BASF SE** (Germany): CEO Markus Kamieth leads the world's largest chemical company. Ludwigshafen Verbund site (world's largest integrated chemical complex) consumes massive natural gas volumes — BASF warned of shutdown risk during 2022 European gas crisis. Wintershall Dea (BASF subsidiary, CEO Mario Mehren) handles E&P. BASF's Nanjing Verbund site (JV with Sinopec) and Zhanjiang integrated complex in Guangdong. BASF Petronas Chemicals (Malaysia JV).
- **Dow Inc.** (USA): CEO Jim Fitterling leads the world's largest polyethylene producer. Freeport (Texas), Plaquemine (Louisiana), Fort Saskatchewan (Alberta) crackers use ethane feedstock from natural gas. Dow's Sadara Chemical Company (JV with Saudi Aramco) in Jubail is the world's largest chemical complex built in a single phase. Dow's Path2Zero project at Fort Saskatchewan targets world's first net-zero ethylene cracker.
- **SABIC** (Saudi Arabia): CEO Abdulrahman Al-Fageeh, 70% owned by Saudi Aramco. World's third-largest diversified chemical company. SABIC's Jubail petrochemical complex, SABIC Agri-Nutrients (fertilizers), SABIC Engineering Plastics (LEXAN, formerly GE Plastics). SABIC Americas (Houston), SABIC Europe (Sittard-Geleen, Netherlands), SABIC Asia Pacific (Shanghai).
- **LyondellBasell** (Netherlands/USA): CEO Peter Vanacker. World's largest polypropylene licensor. Houston refinery, Channelview complex (Texas), Wesseling (Germany), Berre (France) steam crackers. LyondellBasell's circular economy push with MoReTec catalytic recycling.
- **INEOS Group** (UK): Founder and CEO Jim Ratcliffe (also Manchester United investor). INEOS owns Grangemouth Refinery (Scotland's only refinery), Cologne cracker (Germany), Lavera (France), and Project ONE (Antwerp ethane cracker, Europe's largest new cracker in 20 years, using US shale ethane shipped by Dragon-class tankers from Marcus Hook, Pennsylvania). INEOS Styrolution, INEOS Nitriles, INEOS Oxide.
- **ExxonMobil Chemical**: Integrated with ExxonMobil upstream. Baytown Olefins Plant (world's largest), Singapore Chemical Plant, and Baton Rouge Chemical Plant.
- **Shell Chemicals**: Moerdijk (Netherlands), Norco (Louisiana), and Shell Polymers Monaca (Pennsylvania ethane cracker, $6B investment).
- **Formosa Plastics Group** (Taiwan): Founded by the late Wang Yung-ching. Formosa Plastics Corporation (CEO Lin Shen-ren), Nan Ya Plastics (CEO Wu Chia-jui), Formosa Chemicals & Fibre, and Formosa Petrochemical Corporation (Mailiao, Taiwan — one of the world's largest refinery-petrochemical complexes). Formosa's Point Comfort complex in Texas.
- **Reliance Industries** (India): Chairman Mukesh Ambani leads India's largest company. Jamnagar Refinery Complex (Gujarat) — world's largest refining complex at 1.4 million bbl/d (Jamnagar DTA + Jamnagar SEZ). Reliance's petrochemical division produces PTA, MEG, polyester, and polymers. Reliance's oil-to-chemicals transition under CEO Vinder Singh.
- **Sinopec** (China Petroleum & Chemical Corporation): CEO Ma Yongsheng. World's largest refiner by throughput. Sinopec operates 30+ refineries across China including Zhenhai (Ningbo), Yanshan (Beijing), Maoming, Shanghai Gaoqiao, and Qilu. Sinopec's $10B Gulei refinery-petrochemical integrated complex. Sinopec Catalyst Company, Sinopec Engineering Group.
- **PetroChina / CNPC**: Dalian, Fushun, Lanzhou, Dushanzi, Karamay, Daqing, Jilin, and Urumqi refineries. PetroChina Kunlun Energy manages gas distribution.
- **Lotte Chemical** (South Korea): CEO Kim Gyo-hyun. Major naphtha cracker at Yeosu and Daesan. Lotte Chemical Titan (Malaysia), Lotte Chemical USA (Lake Charles ethane cracker JV with Axiall/Westlake Chemical). Lotte's Indonesia petchem investments.
- **Braskem** (Brazil): CEO Roberto Bischoff. Latin America's largest petrochemical company, majority-owned by Novonor (formerly Odebrecht). Triunfo complex (Rio Grande do Sul), Camaçari complex (Bahia). Braskem America operates in La Porte and Marcus Hook.
- **Wanhua Chemical** (China): CEO Liao Zengtai. World's largest MDI (methylene diphenyl diisocyanate) producer, competing with BASF, Covestro (CEO Markus Steilemann), and Huntsman Corporation (CEO Peter Huntsman).
- **Hengli Petrochemical** (China): Chairman Chen Jianhua. Massive integrated refinery-petrochemical complex in Dalian, Liaoning (400,000 bbl/d refinery + PTA + polyester). Connected to Hengli Group textile empire.
- **Zhejiang Petrochemical** (China): Chairman Li Shuirong. Rongsheng Group's 800,000 bbl/d mega-refinery in Zhoushan, built with Saudi Aramco investment. Phase 2 completed. China's largest private refinery.
- **Westlake Chemical**: CEO Albert Chao. Vinyls and olefins producer, Lake Charles (Louisiana) operations. Acquired Boral's North American building products and Hexion's epoxy business.

## Fertilizer Industry (Natural Gas = Primary Feedstock)

Natural gas is the primary feedstock for ammonia and urea production. Gas price spikes directly impact food costs globally.

- **CF Industries** (USA): CEO Tony Will. World's largest nitrogen fertilizer producer. Donaldsonville (Louisiana, world's largest ammonia plant), Port Neal (Iowa), Yazoo City (Mississippi), and Courtright (Ontario). CF Industries' blue ammonia production (with CCS) for clean energy.
- **Yara International** (Norway): CEO Svein Tore Holsether. World's largest ammonia trader. Pilbara green ammonia project (Australia), HEGRA green hydrogen project (Herøya, Norway), and Yara Birkeland (world's first autonomous electric cargo ship). Yara's plants in Porsgrunn (Norway), Sluiskil (Netherlands), Ferrara (Italy), Le Havre (France), and Pilbara (Australia).
- **Nutrien** (Canada): CEO Ken Seitz. World's largest potash producer, formed by Agrium + PotashCorp merger. Nitrogen plants in Redwater, Carseland (Alberta), Augusta (Georgia), Geismar (Louisiana), and Trinidad (Point Lisas). Nutrien Ag Solutions retail network.
- **OCI NV** (Netherlands/Egypt): CEO Ahmed El-Hoshy. OCI operates Iowa Fertilizer Company (IFCo, Wever, Iowa) and Sorfert Algeria (JV with Sonatrach). OCI Beaumont (Texas methanol). Fertiglobe (ADNOC JV, CEO Ahmed El-Hoshy) manages Egypt, UAE, and Algeria assets. OCI Clean Ammonia project.
- **Koch Fertilizers**: Charles Koch's subsidiary, operates Enid (Oklahoma), Fort Dodge (Iowa), and Sterlington (Louisiana) nitrogen plants.
- **EuroChem Group**: Founded by Andrei Melnichenko (sanctioned Russian oligarch). Operates Novomoskovsk, Nevinnomyssk (Russia), and the Kingisepp ammonia plant. EuroChem's Antwerp distribution. EuroChem US acquired by separate interests post-sanctions.
- **PhosAgro** (Russia): CEO Mikhail Rybnikov. World's largest producer of high-grade phosphate rock. Cherepovets and Balakovo plants.
- **Mosaic Company** (USA): CEO Bruce Bodine. Major phosphate and potash producer, Florida phosphate operations and Saskatchewan potash mines.
- **Grupa Azoty** (Poland): CEO Adam Leszkiewicz. Central Europe's largest chemical group, ammonia and fertilizer production highly dependent on natural gas prices.
- **IFFCO** (India): MD U.S. Awasthi. World's largest cooperative-owned fertilizer producer. Kalol, Kandla, Phulpur, and Aonla urea plants consume large volumes of imported LNG and domestic gas from ONGC/Oil India.

## Shipping and Maritime (Oil & Gas Tanker Market)

### Tanker Owners and Operators
- **Frontline Ltd** (Bermuda/Norway): CEO Lars Barstad (succeeded Inger Klemp). World's largest VLCC (Very Large Crude Carrier) operator with 70+ vessels. John Fredriksen's Hemen Holdings is the largest shareholder.
- **Euronav NV** (Belgium): Merged into CMB.TECH (Compagnie Maritime Belge, CEO Alexander Saverys). Major VLCC/Suezmax fleet.
- **Teekay Corporation**: CEO Kenneth Hvid. Teekay Tankers (crude), Teekay LNG Partners (rebranded as Seapeak, CEO Mark Kremin — acquired by Stonepeak Partners).
- **DHT Holdings**: CEO Svein Moxnes Harfjeld. VLCC specialist.
- **International Seaways** (INSW): CEO Lois Zabrocky. Crude and product tanker fleet, merged with Diamond S Shipping.
- **Hafnia** (Denmark): CEO Mikael Skov. World's largest product tanker owner.
- **Scorpio Tankers**: CEO Emanuele Lauro. Major product tanker fleet. Part of Scorpio Group (Lolli-Ghetti/Lauro family).
- **Torm plc** (Denmark): CEO Jacob Meldgaard. Product tanker specialist.
- **Nordic American Tankers (NAT)**: CEO Herbjorn Hansson. Suezmax specialist.
- **MISC Berhad** (Malaysia): CEO Rajalingam Subramaniam. Petronas-controlled shipping company, world's largest LNG shipping fleet via MISC's Eaglestar subsidiary. MISC operates AET Tankers (crude/petroleum transport).
- **Maran Tankers Management**: Part of Angelicoussis Group (founder John Angelicoussis's legacy, managed by Maria Angelicoussis). One of the world's largest privately-owned tanker fleets.
- **MOL (Mitsui O.S.K. Lines)**: CEO Takeshi Hashimoto. Major LNG carrier operator.
- **NYK Line (Nippon Yusen)**: CEO Hitoshi Nagasawa. LNG carrier fleet and offshore vessels.
- **K Line (Kawasaki Kisen Kaisha)**: CEO Yukitoshi Masuda. LNG, car carrier, and dry bulk.
- **Navig8 Group**: CEO Nicolas Busch. Chemical and product tanker pool operator.
- **Capital Maritime & Trading**: CEO Evangelos Marinakis (also Nottingham Forest and Olympiacos owner). Crude and dry bulk fleet.
- **Samsung Heavy Industries**: CEO Jung Jin-taek. Builds LNG carriers and FPSOs at Geoje yard.
- **Hyundai Heavy Industries / HD Korea Shipbuilding**: CEO Ka Sam-hyun. World's largest shipbuilder, dominates LNG carrier orders.
- **Daewoo Shipbuilding (DSME / HD Hyundai Heavy Industries)**: Merged into HD Hyundai. Major LNG carrier builder.

### Shipping Industry Bodies
- **Baltic Exchange**: CEO Mark Jackson. Publishes Baltic Dirty Tanker Index (BDTI), Baltic Clean Tanker Index (BCTI), and Baltic Dry Index (BDI). London-headquartered.
- **International Maritime Organization (IMO)**: Secretary General Arsenio Dominguez. IMO 2020 sulfur cap drove fuel oil market restructuring. IMO targets 50% GHG reduction by 2050.
- **BIMCO**: CEO David Loosley. World's largest shipping industry association.
- **Intertanko**: MD Katharina Stanzel. Association of independent tanker owners.

## Utilities and Power Generation (Gas-Dependent)

### European Utilities
- **ENGIE SA** (France): CEO Catherine MacGregor. Europe's largest gas utility. ENGIE manages ~40 GW of power generation, LNG import terminals at Montoir, Fos Tonkin, and Fos Cavaou. Storengy gas storage subsidiary.
- **E.ON SE** (Germany): CEO Leonhard Birnbaum. Europe's largest operator of power distribution grids. E.ON split off Uniper (now German state-owned). E.ON manages 50 million+ customer connections.
- **RWE AG** (Germany): CEO Markus Krebber. Major gas-fired power generation, 40 GW+ portfolio. RWE Supply & Trading (CEO Andree Stracke) is a top European gas trader. RWE acquired Con Edison's Clean Energy Businesses. RWE's Biblis nuclear site decommissioned.
- **EDF (Electricite de France)**: CEO Luc Remont. Fully state-owned since 2023 delisting. 56 nuclear reactors + gas peakers. EDF Trading manages a top-5 European gas/power book. EDF Renewables (CEO Bruno Bensasson).
- **Enel SpA** (Italy): CEO Flavio Cattaneo. World's largest utility by capacity (~90 GW). Enel Green Power (CEO Salvatore Bernabei), Endesa (Spain subsidiary, CEO Jose Bogas), and Enel Americas (Latin America).
- **Iberdrola** (Spain): CEO Jose Ignacio Sanchez Galan. Major offshore wind developer. Scottish Power subsidiary (UK), Avangrid (US, CEO Pedro Azagra), Neoenergia (Brazil).
- **Naturgy** (Spain, formerly Gas Natural Fenosa): CEO Francisco Reynes. Major Spanish gas distributor and LNG importer.
- **OMV AG** (Austria): CEO Alfred Stern. Central European gas company, refining (Schwechat and Burghausen refineries), Borealis chemicals (CEO Thomas Gangl). OMV's gas storage at Schoenkirchen-Reyersdorf.
- **Vattenfall** (Sweden): CEO Anna Borg. Swedish state-owned, major Nordic gas and wind portfolio.
- **Centrica** (UK): CEO Chris O'Shea. Parent of British Gas, manages Rough gas storage field (UK's largest, reopened 2022). Centrica LNG trading via Spirit Energy.
- **SSE plc** (UK): CEO Alistair Phillips-Davies. Major gas-fired and renewable generator. SSE Thermal, SSE Renewables (Dogger Bank, Seagreen wind farms).
- **Fortum** (Finland): CEO Markus Rauramo. Nordic power utility, formerly owned Uniper (divested to German state).
- **Orsted** (Denmark): CEO Mads Nipper. World's largest offshore wind developer, formerly DONG Energy (Danish Oil and Natural Gas). Divested all oil/gas.

### US Utilities
- **NextEra Energy**: CEO John Ketchum. World's largest utility by market cap. Florida Power & Light (FPL), NextEra Energy Resources (largest US wind/solar developer). Gas generation portfolio being gradually replaced.
- **Duke Energy**: CEO Lynn Good. Major gas-fired generation fleet, Carolinas/Indiana. Duke subsidiary Piedmont Natural Gas serves 1.1 million gas customers.
- **Southern Company**: CEO Christopher Womack. Operates Southern Company Gas (30 bcf gas storage, Nicor Gas, Atlanta Gas Light, Southern Natural Gas pipeline). Southern Nuclear manages Vogtle Units 3-4 (newest US nuclear).
- **Dominion Energy**: CEO Robert Blue. Cove Point LNG export terminal (Maryland, 5.25 mtpa — only US East Coast export facility). Virginia Natural Gas distribution.
- **Sempra Energy**: CEO Jeffrey Martin. Parent of SoCalGas (largest US gas utility, 22 million customers), SDG&E, and Sempra Infrastructure (LNG export). Sempra Texas Utilities (Oncor).
- **CenterPoint Energy**: CEO Jason Wells. Houston gas distribution, natural gas operations across 8 US states.
- **Atmos Energy**: CEO Kevin Akers. Pure-play natural gas distribution, 3.4 million customers across 8 states.
- **NiSource**: CEO Lloyd Yates. Columbia Gas of Ohio, Columbia Gas of Virginia, NIPSCO (Northern Indiana). Formerly owned Columbia Pipeline Group (sold to TC Energy).
- **Consolidated Edison**: CEO Timothy Cawley. ConEd serves New York City with gas and electric.
- **National Fuel Gas Company**: CEO David Bauer. Integrated gas company: Seneca Resources (E&P), National Fuel Gas Supply (pipeline), and National Fuel Gas Distribution (western NY/PA).

### Asian Utilities
- **JERA** (Japan): CEO Yukio Kani. World's largest LNG buyer (JV of TEPCO and Chubu Electric). Operates 70 GW of generation capacity. JERA's fuel procurement head manages 35+ mtpa LNG portfolio.
- **Tokyo Gas**: CEO Takashi Uchida. Major Japanese city gas utility and LNG importer.
- **Korea Electric Power Corporation (KEPCO)**: CEO Kim Dong-cheol. South Korea's sole power transmission company. Subsidiaries: Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power, Korea South-East Power, Korea Midland Power, Korea Southern Power, Korea Western Power, Korea East-West Power. All heavily reliant on LNG-fired generation.
- **PTT Public Company** (Thailand): CEO Kongkrapan Intarajang. Thailand's largest energy conglomerate. PTT Exploration & Production (PTTEP), PTT Global Chemical, Thai Oil, IRPC. PTT's Map Ta Phut LNG receiving terminal.
- **NTPC Limited** (India): CEO Gurdeep Singh. India's largest power utility. NTPC expanding gas-fired capacity and seeking long-term LNG supply.
- **GAIL India**: CEO Sandeep Kumar Gupta. India's largest natural gas processing and distribution company. GAIL manages HVJ and DVPL pipeline networks. GAIL's LNG contracts with Sabine Pass (Cheniere), Gazprom, and QatarEnergy.

## Airlines (Jet Fuel — Direct Oil Product)

Airlines are among the most directly impacted by oil price movements through jet fuel (kerosene) costs:

- **Delta Air Lines**: CEO Ed Bastian. Operates Monroe Energy refinery (Trainer, Pennsylvania) — the only airline-owned refinery, processing 185,000 bbl/d.
- **United Airlines**: CEO Scott Kirby. Fuel costs represent ~30% of operating expenses.
- **American Airlines**: CEO Robert Isom. Major jet fuel hedging program.
- **Lufthansa Group**: CEO Carsten Spohr. Includes Swiss, Austrian Airlines, Brussels Airlines, Eurowings. Major SAF purchaser.
- **Air France-KLM**: CEO Benjamin Smith. European fuel cost pressure.
- **British Airways** (IAG subsidiary): CEO Sean Doyle. IAG (CEO Luis Gallego) hedges fuel.
- **Emirates**: President Tim Clark. Dubai hub, major kerosene buyer.
- **Singapore Airlines**: CEO Goh Choon Phong. Premium carrier with high fuel sensitivity.
- **Cathay Pacific**: CEO Ronald Lam. Hong Kong-based, major Asian fuel buyer.
- **Ryanair**: CEO Michael O'Leary. Europe's largest carrier by passengers. Aggressive fuel hedging strategy.
- **IATA** (International Air Transport Association): Director General Willie Walsh. Publishes jet fuel price monitor.

## Metals and Mining (Energy-Intensive, Connected via Power and Diesel)

- **BHP Group**: CEO Mike Henry. World's largest mining company. Oil/gas divested to Woodside Energy in 2022. Copper (Escondida, Olympic Dam), iron ore (Pilbara), and potash (Jansen, Canada). Diesel and natural gas are primary energy inputs.
- **Rio Tinto**: CEO Jakob Stausholm. Major aluminum smelting (Kitimat, Boyne, Tiwai Point) — aluminum is extremely energy-intensive. Rio Tinto Alcan legacy. Iron ore (Pilbara), copper (Oyu Tolgoi, Mongolia with Turquoise Hill).
- **Vale SA** (Brazil): CEO Eduardo Bartolomeo. World's largest iron ore producer. Iron ore ships are the largest dry bulk vessels (Valemax class), burning heavy fuel oil.
- **Anglo American**: CEO Duncan Wanblad. Diversified mining: De Beers (diamonds), Kumba Iron Ore, Anglo American Platinum (Amplats), Quellaveco copper (Peru).
- **Alcoa Corporation**: CEO William Oplinger. Major aluminum producer, smelters are among the most energy-intensive industrial facilities globally. Alcoa's San Ciprian (Spain) smelter faced shutdown threats from high energy costs.
- **Norsk Hydro** (Norway): CEO Hilde Merete Aasheim. Aluminum smelting powered by Norwegian hydropower — competitive advantage vs gas-dependent smelters.
- **Freeport-McMoRan**: CEO Richard Adkerson. World's largest publicly traded copper producer. Grasberg (Indonesia, with PT Indonesia Asahan Aluminium INALUM).
- **Glencore**: CEO Gary Nagle. Coal trading (world's largest), copper, zinc, nickel. Glencore's coal exit pressure from activists vs strong cash generation.
- **Newmont Corporation**: CEO Tom Palmer. World's largest gold miner. Diesel costs major input. Peñasquito, Boddington, Tanami, Ahafo operations.
- **ArcelorMittal**: CEO Aditya Mittal. World's second-largest steelmaker. Natural gas is primary feedstock for DRI (direct reduced iron). ArcelorMittal Dofasco (Hamilton, Ontario) switching to hydrogen-DRI.
- **Nucor Corporation**: CEO Leon Topalian. Largest US steelmaker. Electric arc furnaces use massive electricity (often gas-generated).
- **US Steel**: CEO David Burritt. Acquired by Nippon Steel (CEO Eiji Hashimoto) pending regulatory approval by CFIUS (Committee on Foreign Investment, chaired by Treasury Secretary).
