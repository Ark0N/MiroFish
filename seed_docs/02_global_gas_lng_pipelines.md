# Global Natural Gas, LNG, and Pipeline Infrastructure (March 2026)

## LNG Liquefaction — Major Export Terminals

### United States (World's Largest LNG Exporter)
The US became the world's largest LNG exporter in 2023 and continues to expand capacity:

- **Sabine Pass LNG** (Cameron Parish, Louisiana): Operated by Cheniere Energy (CEO Jack Fusco), 6 trains totaling 30 mtpa. Cheniere subsidiary Sabine Pass Liquefaction LLC operates the facility. Major offtakers include Shell, Korea Gas Corporation (KOGAS), Gas Natural Fenosa (Naturgy), Centrica, Total, and GAIL India.
- **Cameron LNG** (Hackberry, Louisiana): Operated by Cameron LNG Company (Sempra Energy subsidiary, CEO Jeffrey Martin). 3 trains at 15 mtpa. Partners include TotalEnergies, Mitsubishi Corporation, and Mitsui & Co. Train 4 expansion under development by Sempra Infrastructure (CEO Justin Bird).
- **Corpus Christi LNG** (Texas): Cheniere Energy's second facility. Stage 3 expansion adds 10+ mtpa. CEO Jack Fusco targets 45+ mtpa total Cheniere portfolio. Long-term contracts with ENN Energy (China), PetroChina, Foran Energy Group, EDF Trading, and Inpex.
- **Freeport LNG** (Quintana Island, Texas): Operated by Freeport LNG Development LP, led by CEO Michael Smith. 3 trains at 15 mtpa. Investors include Osaka Gas, JERA (Japan's largest power generator, JV of TEPCO and Chubu Electric), and BP. Freeport experienced a 2022 explosion and prolonged restart.
- **Elba Island LNG** (Georgia): Southern Company subsidiary Southern LNG, operated by Kinder Morgan (CEO Kim Dang). Small-scale modular trains totaling 2.5 mtpa.
- **Calcasieu Pass LNG** (Cameron Parish, Louisiana): Venture Global LNG (CEO Mike Sestal) operates this 10 mtpa facility, the first to use a mid-scale modular approach. Buyers include Shell, BP, Edison SpA, Repsol, Galp Energia, PGNiG (now Orlen), and Uniper.
- **Plaquemines LNG** (Louisiana): Venture Global's second facility under construction, targeting 20 mtpa in two phases. Offtakers include New Fortress Energy (CEO Wesley Edens), China Gas Holdings, ExxonMobil, Chevron, ConocoPhillips, EnBW (Germany), and SEFE (formerly Gazprom Germania, now German state-owned).
- **Golden Pass LNG** (Sabine Pass, Texas): QatarEnergy (70%) and ExxonMobil (30%) JV, converting former import terminal to export. 3 trains at 18 mtpa. Managed by Chiyoda Corporation and Zachry Group (project faced delays and Zachry's bankruptcy).
- **Port Arthur LNG** (Texas): Sempra Infrastructure facility, 13 mtpa Phase 1. Offtakers include ConocoPhillips and RWE (Germany, CEO Markus Krebber).
- **Rio Grande LNG** (Brownsville, Texas): NextDecade Corporation (CEO Matt Schatzman), 27 mtpa planned. Train 1-3 FID taken. Offtakers include Shell, TotalEnergies, Guangdong Energy, Itochu, Engie, and Galp.
- **Driftwood LNG** (Louisiana): Tellurian Inc (now rebranded/restructured under Woodside Energy after acquisition). Originally Charif Souki's project. 27.6 mtpa planned.
- **Alaska LNG**: Long-delayed Alaska Gasline Development Corporation (AGDC) project to monetize Prudhoe Bay and Point Thomson gas. Governor Mike Dunleavy supports. Partners sought from KOGAS and Sinopec.
- **Commonwealth LNG** (Cameron Parish, Louisiana): 9.3 mtpa planned, CEO Paul Shortridge.
- **Magnolia LNG** (Louisiana): Glenfarne Group (CEO Brendan Duval), 8.8 mtpa.
- **Woodfibre LNG** (British Columbia): Pacific Energy Corporation (owned by Singapore's RGE Group, Sukanto Tanoto), 2.1 mtpa.

### Qatar (World's Largest LNG Capacity)
QatarEnergy, led by CEO and Energy Minister Saad Sherida Al-Kaabi, is executing the biggest LNG expansion in history:
- **North Field East (NFE)**: 4 mega-trains adding 32 mtpa. Partners: TotalEnergies (25%), Shell (25%), ConocoPhillips (12.5%), Eni (12.5%), ExxonMobil (12.5%). Contractor: Chiyoda-Technip Energies JV.
- **North Field South (NFS)**: 2 additional trains adding 16 mtpa. Partners: TotalEnergies, Shell, ConocoPhillips, Eni. Total Qatar LNG to reach 126 mtpa by 2028.
- **QatarGas** (merged into QatarEnergy LNG): Operating trains at Ras Laffan Industrial City. Ras Laffan managed by CEO Ali bin Ahmed Al-Kuwari.
- **Qatargas 1**: Partnership with ExxonMobil, TotalEnergies, Marubeni, and Mitsui.
- **Qatargas 2**: Partnership with ExxonMobil.
- **RasGas** (legacy trains merged): Partnership with ExxonMobil.
- Long-term offtakers: CPC Corporation Taiwan, JERA, Tohoku Electric, Kansai Electric, Korea Gas Corporation, Sinopec, PetroChina, CNOOC, Petronet LNG (India), Bangladesh Oil, Gas & Mineral Corp (Petrobangla), Pakistan LNG Limited, and numerous European utilities.

### Australia
Australia is the world's third-largest LNG exporter:
- **North West Shelf LNG** (Karratha, WA): Woodside Energy (CEO Meg O'Neill) operates for the NWS JV (Woodside, Shell, BP, Chevron, BHP, JERA). 6 trains at 16.7 mtpa.
- **Gorgon LNG** (Barrow Island, WA): Chevron-operated, partners Shell and ExxonMobil. 3 trains at 15.6 mtpa. Carbon injection system (world's largest CCS) has underperformed.
- **Wheatstone LNG** (Ashburton, WA): Chevron-operated, partners KUFPEC (Kuwait), Woodside, Kyushu Electric, and JERA.
- **Ichthys LNG** (Darwin, NT): Inpex Corporation (CEO Takayuki Ueda) operates, with TotalEnergies as partner. 8.9 mtpa.
- **Australia Pacific LNG (APLNG)** (Gladstone, QLD): ConocoPhillips (37.5%), Origin Energy (CEO Frank Calabria, 27.5%), Sinopec (25%), APA Group pipeline (10%).
- **Gladstone LNG (GLNG)** (Gladstone, QLD): Santos Limited (CEO Kevin Gallagher) operates, partners Petronas, TotalEnergies, and KOGAS.
- **Queensland Curtis LNG (QCLNG)** (Gladstone, QLD): Shell-operated, partner CNOOC and Tokyo Gas.
- **Prelude FLNG** (Browse Basin): Shell's floating facility, 3.6 mtpa.
- **Pluto LNG** (Karratha, WA): Woodside Energy, single train 4.9 mtpa. Pluto Train 2 expansion linked to Scarborough gas development.
- **Scarborough Project**: Woodside Energy's $12B gas development feeding Pluto Train 2. Partners include JERA and LNG Japan (JOGMEC/Mitsubishi).
- **Browse FLNG**: Long-delayed Woodside project, partners Shell, BP, JERA, and PetroChina.
- **Barossa Project**: Santos-operated gas field feeding Darwin LNG (replacing depleted Bayu-Undan). Partner SK E&S (South Korea, CEO Choo Hyeong-wook).

### Africa
- **Nigeria LNG** (Bonny Island): NLNG (NNPC 49%, Shell 25.6%, TotalEnergies 15%, Eni 10.4%). 6 trains at 22 mtpa. Train 7 (8 mtpa expansion) under construction, EPC by Saipem and Daewoo E&C (now HD Hyundai E&C).
- **Angola LNG** (Soyo): Chevron-operated (36.4%), Sonangol, BP, Eni, TotalEnergies.
- **Egypt LNG** (Idku): Shell/Petronas operated. BG Group legacy asset.
- **ELNG** (Damietta): Eni/Union Fenosa Gas (Naturgy).
- **Mozambique LNG** (Area 1): TotalEnergies-led, 12.9 mtpa. Delayed by Cabo Delgado insurgency. Partners: ONGC Videsh, Bharat PetroResources, PTTEP, Mitsui, ENH.
- **Coral South FLNG** (Mozambique Area 4): Eni-operated floating facility, 3.4 mtpa. Partners: ExxonMobil, CNPC, KOGAS, Galp, ENH.
- **Greater Tortue Ahmeyim** (Mauritania/Senegal): BP-operated FLNG, 2.3 mtpa Phase 1. Partners: Kosmos Energy (CEO Andrew Inglis), SMH (Mauritania), Petrosen (Senegal). Technip Energies built the FLNG vessel.
- **Senegal LNG developments**: Woodside Energy's Sangomar oil project offshore Senegal recently started production.
- **Tanzania LNG**: Long-proposed project by Equinor and Shell using Block 1/4 deepwater gas. Tanzania Petroleum Development Corporation (TPDC) partner.
- **Algeria Arzew complex**: Sonatrach operates GL1K, GL2Z, GL3Z, and GL4Z at Arzew and Skikda LNG.

### Southeast Asia
- **Malaysia**: Petronas LNG Complex (Bintulu, Sarawak) — 9 trains totaling 30 mtpa. PETRONAS (CEO Muhammad Taufik), world's third-largest LNG producer. Petronas Floating LNG 1 (PFLNG1 Satu) and PFLNG2 Dua deployed to deepwater fields. Petronas Carigali (E&P arm). Partners include Shell Sarawak, and Murphy Oil.
- **Indonesia**: BP Tangguh LNG (Papua, 2 trains + Train 3 expansion). Pertamina (CEO Nicke Widyawati) manages Bontang LNG and the Abadi FLNG project (with Inpex). Donggi-Senoro LNG (Mitsubishi, Pertamina, Medco Energi).
- **Papua New Guinea**: PNG LNG (ExxonMobil-operated, Santos, JX Nippon, Kumul Petroleum Holdings CEO Wapu Sonk). Papua LNG (TotalEnergies-led expansion, 5.4 mtpa).

## Major Gas Pipelines and Networks

### North America
- **Permian Highway Pipeline**: Kinder Morgan (CEO Kim Dang), EagleClaw Midstream (Blackstone portfolio), Kinetik Holdings. 2.65 bcf/d Permian to Gulf Coast.
- **Whistler Pipeline**: WhiteWater Midstream (CEO Christer Rundlof), MPLX LP, and DT Midstream. Permian to Agua Dulce hub.
- **Mountain Valley Pipeline (MVP)**: Equitrans Midstream (now merged into EQT Corporation, CEO Toby Rice). 2 bcf/d Appalachian gas to Southeast US. Controversial, completed 2024 after years of legal battles.
- **TC Energy pipelines**: TC Energy Corporation (CEO Francois Poirier) operates NGTL (Alberta), Mainline (Trans-Canada), ANR Pipeline, Columbia Gas Transmission, and Coastal GasLink (feeding LNG Canada, now under Pembina Pipeline or TC Energy). TC Energy's South Bow spinoff handles oil pipelines.
- **Williams Companies**: CEO Alan Armstrong operates the Transco pipeline (largest US gas pipeline system, Gulf Coast to Northeast), Northwest Pipeline, and Gulfstream (with Southern Company). Williams' ~15% of US gas processing.
- **Energy Transfer**: CEO Kelcy Warren operates a vast pipeline network including Panhandle Eastern, Rover Pipeline, Mariner East (NGL), and the 2 bcf/d Permian Express system. Energy Transfer Equity/Partners restructured.
- **ONEOK**: CEO Pierce Norton operates NGL pipelines from Bakken and Permian to Mont Belvieu hub. Recently acquired Magellan Midstream Partners.
- **Enterprise Products Partners**: CEO Jim Teague operates the world's largest NGL pipeline network, fractionation at Mont Belvieu, and ECHO crude terminal.
- **DT Midstream**: CEO David Slater operates Haynesville gathering and Blue Union pipeline system.
- **Targa Resources**: CEO Matt Meloy operates Permian and Louisiana gathering/processing.
- **Crestwood Equity Partners**: Merged into Energy Transfer.
- **Western Midstream Partners**: Formerly Anadarko, now Occidental-controlled.

### Europe
- **Nord Stream 1**: Sabotaged September 2022. Operator Nord Stream AG. Gazprom (51%), Wintershall Dea (now BASF subsidiary, CEO Mario Mehren), E.ON/Uniper, Gasunie (Netherlands), ENGIE.
- **Nord Stream 2**: Never commissioned after Russia-Ukraine war. Operator Nord Stream 2 AG. Funded by Gazprom, Shell, OMV (Austria, CEO Alfred Stern), ENGIE (France, CEO Catherine MacGregor), Wintershall Dea, Uniper.
- **TurkStream**: Gazprom's pipeline to Turkey (and onward to Southeast Europe via Bulgaria). 31.5 bcm/yr capacity.
- **TANAP** (Trans-Anatolian Pipeline): Turkey-Azerbaijan pipeline, 16 bcm/yr expandable to 32. SOCAR (51%), BOTAŞ (Turkey, 30%), BP (12%), TPAO (7%).
- **TAP** (Trans Adriatic Pipeline): Greece-Albania-Italy, 10 bcm/yr expandable to 20. Partners: BP (20%), SOCAR (20%), Snam SpA (CEO Stefano Venier, 20%), Fluxys Belgium (19%), ENAGÁS (Spain, CEO Arturo Gonzalo, 16%), Axpo (Switzerland, 5%).
- **Baltic Pipe**: Poland-Denmark-Norway pipeline, 10 bcm/yr. Operated by GAZ-SYSTEM (Poland, CEO Slawomir Hinc) and Energinet (Denmark).
- **EastMed Pipeline**: Proposed Israel-Cyprus-Greece pipeline. Energean (CEO Mathios Rigas) develops Karish gas field feeding it. DEPA (Greece, CEO Konstantinos Xifaras) involved. Recently deprioritized in favor of Eastern Mediterranean hydrogen.
- **Interconnector Greece-Bulgaria (IGB)**: ICGB AD, linking TAP gas to Bulgaria. Bulgartransgaz (CEO Vladimir Malinov) operator.
- **MidCat / BarMar / H2Med**: Proposed Spain-France hydrogen/gas link. ENAGAS and GRTgaz (France, subsidiary of ENGIE) involved.
- **European gas hubs**: TTF (Netherlands, operated by Gasunie subsidiary GTS, CEO Han Fennema), NBP (UK, National Grid CEO John Pettigrew), CEGH (Austria, Central European Gas Hub).
- **Snam SpA** (Italy): Europe's largest gas pipeline operator, 32,700 km. CEO Stefano Venier. Operates TAG (Trans Austria Gasleitung) pipeline system.
- **ENGIE SA**: CEO Catherine MacGregor manages Europe's largest gas utility, including Storengy (gas storage, largest in Europe), GRDF (French gas distribution), and major LNG import terminals.
- **Fluxys** (Belgium): CEO Pascal De Buck operates Zeebrugge LNG terminal and extensive pipeline network.

### Middle East and Central Asia
- **Dolphin Pipeline**: Dolphin Energy (Mubadala Investment Company, TotalEnergies, Occidental) transports Qatari gas to UAE and Oman. 3.2 bcf/d.
- **Iran-Turkey gas pipeline**: Operated by BOTAŞ and NIGC. Frequent disputes over price and volume.
- **Turkmenistan-China pipeline**: Central Asia-China Gas Pipeline (Line A, B, C), operated by CNPC. 55 bcm/yr. Line D planned through Tajikistan and Kyrgyzstan. Turkmengas (CEO Maksat Babayev) supplies from Galkynysh field (world's second-largest gas field).
- **TAPI Pipeline** (Turkmenistan-Afghanistan-Pakistan-India): Long-proposed 33 bcm/yr project. Turkmengas leads. Afghan section faces security concerns.
- **Power of Siberia**: Gazprom pipeline to China (Chayandinskoye and Kovyktinskoye fields to Blagoveshchensk border). 38 bcm/yr at full capacity. CNPC subsidiary PipeChina (CEO Zhang Wei) receives at Chinese border.
- **Power of Siberia 2**: Proposed Mongolia transit route, 50 bcm/yr. Requires approval from Mongolian President Ukhnaagiin Khurelsukh.

### Asia-Pacific LNG Import Terminals
- **Japan**: JERA (CEO Yukio Kani) is the world's largest LNG buyer. Other importers: Tokyo Gas (CEO Takashi Uchida), Osaka Gas (CEO Masataka Fujiwara), Toho Gas, Tohoku Electric Power, Kyushu Electric, Shizuoka Gas, Hiroshima Gas, INPEX. Japan has 35+ LNG regasification terminals.
- **South Korea**: Korea Gas Corporation KOGAS (CEO Choi Yeonhye) operates Incheon, Tongyeong, Samcheok, and Pyeongtaek terminals. SK E&S, POSCO International (CEO Jeong Tak), and GS Energy also import.
- **China**: PetroChina (CNPC subsidiary, CEO Hou Qijun) operates Rudong, Dalian, Tangshan, and Caofeidian terminals. CNOOC (CEO Wang Dongjin) operates Dapeng (Guangdong), Fujian, Zhejiang, Tianjin, and Hainan terminals. Sinopec (CEO Ma Yongsheng) operates Qingdao and Beihai terminals. ENN Energy (CEO Wang Yusuo), Beijing Gas (CEO Li Yalan), Guanghui Energy, China Gas Holdings, and Zhejiang Energy import via multiple terminals.
- **India**: Petronet LNG (CEO Akshay Kumar Singh) operates Dahej (world's largest, 17.5 mtpa) and Kochi terminals. Other terminals: Dhamra (GAIL, CEO Sandeep Kumar Gupta, and BPCL), Mundra (GSPC), Ennore (Indian Oil), Jafrabad (Swan Energy). India Gas Exchange (IGX) is the country's first gas trading platform.
- **Taiwan**: CPC Corporation (CEO Li Shunqin) operates Yung-An and Taichung LNG terminals.
- **Bangladesh**: Petrobangla contracted Excelerate Energy (CEO Steven Kobos) for FSRU-based imports at Moheshkhali. Summit Group (CEO Muhammed Aziz Khan) also operates FSRUs.
- **Pakistan**: Engro Corporation and Gunvor Group import via Engro Elengy FSRU (Port Qasim). Pakistan LNG Limited and Tabeer Energy FSRU at Port Qasim.
- **Thailand**: PTTEP (CEO Montri Rawanchaikul) and PTT Public Company (CEO Kongkrapan Intarajang) operate Map Ta Phut LNG terminal.
- **Singapore**: Singapore LNG Corporation operates Jurong Island terminal. SLNG receives cargoes from major portfolio players.
- **Philippines**: First Gen Corporation (CEO Federico Lopez) developing Batangas LNG terminal to replace depleted Malampaya gas field (Shell-operated).

## European Gas Storage
- **Uniper SE** (Germany): CEO Michael Lewis (post-nationalization). Major gas storage at Etzel, Bierwang, Huntorf, and the massive Haidach facility (Austria border). Uniper's trading arm is one of Europe's largest.
- **Storengy** (ENGIE subsidiary): Operates 21 storage sites in France, Germany, and UK. CEO Cécile Prévieu.
- **RWE Supply & Trading**: CEO Andree Stracke manages a major gas trading and storage book. RWE's Dutch storage at Bergermeer.
- **MVM Group** (Hungary): State utility managing Hungarian gas storage and Russian pipeline gas imports. CEO Gyorgy Kocsis.
- **Naftogaz** (Ukraine): CEO Oleksiy Chernyshov manages Europe's largest gas storage capacity (31 bcm at facilities including Bilche-Volytsko-Uherske). Transit of Russian gas ended January 2025 when Naftogaz declined to renew the Gazprom transit contract.
- **Interconnector UK**: Connects UK gas system with Zeebrugge (Belgium). Operated by Interconnector Ltd (CEO Dirk De Maeseneer).
- **Gas Infrastructure Europe (GIE)**: Secretary General Torben Brabo tracks pan-European storage levels.
- **ENTSOG** (European Network of TSOs for Gas): CEO Jan Ingwersen coordinates cross-border gas flows.
